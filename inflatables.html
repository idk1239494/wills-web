What are inflatables?


Inflatables are fabric objects made up of mostly heavy duty nylon silk or polyester. Every inflatable has a unique shape to express something, particularly for decorative purposes. They can be shaped into characters, objects, or anything else. Inflatables are just piles of fabric without a fan. The fan is a blower on the back, which is where the cord is. Once plugged in, the blades inside the fan will start spinning, and will begin blowing air into the inflatable, thus causing it to 'inflate,' until it is fully inflated. Almost all outdoor inflatables have one or more light to make them glow in the dark. The most common lights are LEDs (cheap fans) and bulbs (base fan). The lights turn on once the fan receives electricity, because the fan shares the electricity via a wire inside the inflatable, causing it to turn on. Some lights may be colored, or some lights can be flashing lights to make it stand out more. Most new inflatables have fabric in good condition, but old ones tend to be more worn, and the fabric is more soft and silky. Most inflatables are made by Gemmy Industries, or more simply known as Gemmy.

Cheap Fan vs. Base Fan


Cheap fans are small fans meant for 2-7ft inflatables. Cheap fans come in different sizes and take up a different amount of volts. The weakest cheap fan is only about 2 inches wide. The medium cheap fan is about 3 inches. The strongest is about 4 inches. Finding the strongest cheap fan is actually quite hard. Base fans however, are a new league. Base fans are meant for inflatables 7-11ft tall. Most base fans are all the same strength and size, which is 10 inches. Base fans are much stronger and louder than cheap fans. Unlike cheap fans, base fan inflatables do not use LEDs, but use bulbs that are inside the inflatable loosely connected to the fan and the top of the inside, not connected by the inflatable’s wall. However, they both do work the same way of blowing air.

Unique Inflatables


Some inflatables have extra utilities that make them different from bland inflatables. They may have real hair, be animated, have flashing lights, or many other things. One example is a shivering animation. Shivering inflatables are by far the most common animated inflatables. Shivering inflatables work the same way their lights work, the fan shares electricity through a wire to the lights, but also to a mechanic inside the inflatable. When this mechanic turns on, an object attached to a motor on the inside of the mechanic will begin to spin quickly. This causes the outside to vigorously shake, thus creating a ‘shivering’ motion within the inflatable. Another type is an inflatable that has flashing lights, where the lights turn off and to stand and pop out more.

Rare Inflatables


Rare inflatables are inflatables that are very hard to find today or are no longer readily available. These inflatables can be from 2013-2002. Rare inflatables are almost always worn down, and their boxes are most likely damaged. The fan also might sound different due to it’s age. Rare inflatables may come from any company, but most of the time they are from Gemmy. Some rare inflatables I have is a 3.5 foot snowman, 3.5 foot witch, Red from angry birds with a santa hat, a Hello Kitty with a dress and wings, and many more. But the inflatable that is possibly my rarest is a very simple inflatable mummy holding a sign that says ‘Happy Halloween,’ pictured below.

As you can see, the mummy is so simple to the point it kind of looks like a ghost. Though not visible in the photo, the top of the mummy’s head is stained with something, and it feels very silky and soft, much like all other old inflatables. I could only find 6 photos of this mummy that exists, and none of them were selling the mummy. And all of the mummies in the photos also had the flipped letters in the sign like the one I have, which might have been an error, but who knows. There could be only a few of these mummies today, and I found out that buying one of these today is very, very hard.

Air Dancers


Air dancers are a type of inflatable that are much different than holiday outdoor yard inflatables. Air dancers are fabric heavy duty nylon tubes, almost all of the time with a smiling face and arms. There is a hole at the bottom and top of the fabric tube body, and at the top there is fabric strips of thick hair. There may also be holes in the arms. Like all inflatables, without a source of air, it is useless. But air dancers don’t have just a fan, they have a very big high-voltage blower. An air dancer blower is much stronger and louder than cheap and base fans, and require to be switched on via a switch and not just plugged in. The air dancer would be attached to the blower by the bottom via velcro. Once plugged in, the blower blows a very strong rush of air that inflates the tube body. However, due to the hole in the top, it deflates at the same time due to the air rapidly escaping the tube body, so it will begin falling down, but as it does, the air from the blower will continue to blow upward, causing it to fold over and yank straight up again. This process continues until it is unplugged. Unlike holiday inflatables, which are used for decorative purposes, air dancers are primarily used for advertising and attracting customers in businesses.


Old air dancers tend to be worn down, with their colors dull and not as vibrant, and sometimes are ripped. Their face can be worn down too, and the entire tube body might also be dirty. Rare air dancers are very hard to find, as they last a bit shorter than airblown inflatables. Air dancers come in many heights, mostly 6 ft, 10 ft, or 20 ft. Some air dancers are shorter than 6 ft, and some are taller than 20 ft, in one instance an air dancer was 35 ft. The bigger the air dancer is, the wider the tube body gets, and on top of that, the blower gets stronger each size. 6 feet blowers are small and moderately heavy and provide decent blowing. 10 feet blowers are slightly larger and more heavier and provide a stronger blow of air. 20 feet blowers are very very big, their top being about 10-11 inches. They are so heavy, most have wheels on the bottom so they can be rolled around. They provide a huge amount of air, and are very strong. I have all of the. 3 sizes, 1 is 6 feet, 3 are 10 feet, and another 3 are 20 feet. The 6 ft one is a red and yellow one nicknamed ‘Red sausage and Cheese,’ shown above. The 10 ft ones are a yellow air dancer with eyelashes, a cactus-themed air dancer, and an air dancer no arms or face, with red, white, and blue from top to bottom in the colors of the French flag, nicknamed ‘French Revolution.’ The 3 20 feet ones are a blue air dancer with red hair, a ghost-themed air dancer, and an orange air dancer with eyelashes.

Bounce Houses


Bounce houses are large inflatable castle-shaped fabric, almost always multicolored. Bounce houses are neither for decorative or advertisement purposes. Rather, they are mostly meant for celebration, like birthday parties. Bounce houses are powered by a strong blower, which blows air into the inflatable via a fabric tube attached to the fan. The air inside the inflatable will make the floor of the inside bouncy, thus being named ‘bounce houses.’ Bounce houses can vary in size, some being giant inflatable water slides. I have one bounce house, which was given to us, shown above. It is a simple one Old bounce houses tend to be very worn down and might have a few stains and rips. They are mostly built with very strong and relatively thick fabric to whist and the weight of a person.
